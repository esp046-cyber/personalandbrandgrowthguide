Terminologies & Glossary

üìö Browse by Category:

Social Media Terms

Algorithm
The set of rules and calculations platforms use to determine what content to show users and in what order. Each platform has its own algorithm that considers factors like engagement, recency, and relevance.
Engagement Rate
The percentage of your audience that interacts with your content. Formula: (Likes + Comments + Shares + Saves) √∑ Followers √ó 100. A good engagement rate is 3-6% for most platforms.
Reach vs Impressions
Reach: Number of unique accounts that saw your content.
Impressions: Total number of times your content was displayed (one person can create multiple impressions).
CTR (Click-Through Rate)
Percentage of people who clicked on a link after seeing your post. Formula: Clicks √∑ Impressions √ó 100. Good CTR varies by platform but generally 1-3% is decent.
Viral
Content that spreads rapidly through shares and engagement, reaching far beyond your normal audience. Typically defined as content reaching 10x+ your normal reach.
Shadow Ban
When a platform limits your content's visibility without notifying you. Your posts appear normal to you but are hidden from most of your audience. Usually caused by violating community guidelines or suspicious activity.
Bot / Fake Followers
Automated or fake accounts that follow you but don't engage. They inflate follower counts but hurt engagement rate. Avoid buying followers - platforms can detect and penalize this.
Handle / Username
Your unique identifier on a platform (e.g., @yourname). Should be consistent across platforms for easy discovery and brand recognition.

Marketing Terms

üéØCore Marketing Concepts

Funnel: The journey people take from discovering you to becoming customers. Stages: Awareness ‚Üí Interest ‚Üí Decision ‚Üí Action.

Lead Magnet: Free valuable resource (guide, checklist, template) offered in exchange for email address. Used to build email list.

Conversion: When someone takes desired action - subscribe, purchase, book call, etc. Conversion rate = percentage who complete action.

SEO (Search Engine Optimization): Optimizing content to rank higher in search results. Involves keywords, quality content, backlinks, and technical factors.

Analytics: Data about your content performance - views, engagement, demographics, etc. Used to make informed decisions.

KPI (Key Performance Indicator): Metrics that matter most for your goals. Examples: engagement rate, email list growth, revenue per follower.

ROI (Return on Investment): Profit gained relative to money/time invested. Formula: (Gain - Cost) √∑ Cost √ó 100

Niche: Specific segment of market you serve. Intersection of your expertise, passion, and audience need. Being specific helps you stand out.
Target Audience
Specific group of people most likely to benefit from and purchase your products/services. Defined by demographics (age, location), psychographics (values, interests), and behaviors.
Value Proposition
Clear statement of the unique value you provide and why someone should follow/buy from you instead of competitors. Answers: "What problem do you solve and why are you the best choice?"
USP (Unique Selling Proposition)
What makes you different from everyone else in your space. Your unique combination of expertise, personality, approach, or results you deliver.
A/B Testing
Testing two versions of content to see which performs better. Example: posting same content with different headlines to see which gets more engagement.

Content Creation Terms

Evergreen Content
Content that remains relevant and valuable over time (opposite of trending/timely content). Examples: tutorials, how-to guides, foundational concepts. Continues to attract audience long after posting.
UGC (User-Generated Content)
Content created by your audience about your brand - reviews, testimonials, photos using your product, etc. Highly valuable as social proof and builds community.
Carousel
Multi-image post that users swipe through (Instagram/LinkedIn). Great for step-by-step guides, lists, before-and-after, or educational content. Higher engagement than single images.
Reel / TikTok
Short-form vertical video content (15-90 seconds). Highly favored by algorithms. Best for quick tips, entertaining content, trending formats, or behind-the-scenes.
Thread
Series of connected posts/tweets telling a story or explaining a concept. Popular on Twitter/X. Allows you to go deeper than single post limit.
Hook
Opening line or first 1-3 seconds of content designed to grab attention and make people stop scrolling. Critical for performance since most people scroll quickly.
CTA (Call-to-Action)
Specific instruction telling audience what to do next. Examples: "Follow for more", "Link in bio", "Comment YES", "Save this post". Every post should have one.
Story / Highlight
Story: Temporary content (24 hours) shown at top of feed. Good for casual, frequent updates.
Highlight: Permanent collection of saved Stories on your profile. Organized by topic for new visitors.
Content Pillars
3-5 main topics you consistently create content about. Helps maintain focus and positions you as expert. Example: Fitness creator's pillars might be Workouts, Nutrition, Mindset, Recovery.
Repurposing
Taking one piece of content and reformatting it for multiple platforms or formats. Example: Blog post ‚Üí Twitter thread ‚Üí Instagram carousel ‚Üí Email newsletter ‚Üí YouTube video.

Business & Monetization Terms

üí∞Revenue Models

Monetization: Process of earning money from your personal brand. Multiple streams recommended: products, services, sponsorships, affiliates.

Affiliate Marketing: Earning commission by promoting other companies' products. You get unique link, earn 5-50% per sale. Amazon Associates, ShareASale are examples.

Sponsorship: Brand pays you to create content featuring their product/service. Usually one-time payment per post or campaign.

Media Kit: Professional document showcasing your stats, audience demographics, services, and rates. Sent to brands when pitching partnerships.

Rate Card: Your pricing sheet for different services (post, story, video, etc.). Updated as you grow. Start rates based on follower count and engagement.

Digital Product: Downloadable product like ebook, template, course, or guide. Created once, sold repeatedly. High profit margin.

MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue): Predictable income from subscriptions or memberships. Most sustainable business model. Examples: Patreon, membership sites.

Coaching/Consulting: Selling your time and expertise 1-on-1 or in groups. Higher ticket pricing ($500-$5000+) but limited by your time.
Lead
Potential customer who has shown interest by sharing contact info (usually email). Quality leads are people who match your target audience and have engagement intent.
Email List
Collection of email addresses from people interested in your content. Most valuable asset - you own it (unlike social followers). Average email list subscriber worth $1-$10/month.
Sales Funnel
Automated sequence moving people from awareness to purchase. Example: Free lead magnet ‚Üí Welcome emails ‚Üí Educational content ‚Üí Offer ‚Üí Purchase ‚Üí Upsell.
Landing Page
Single-purpose webpage designed for specific action - sign up, buy, register. No navigation distractions. Optimized for conversion.
Upsell / Cross-sell
Upsell: Offering higher-priced version or add-on after initial purchase.
Cross-sell: Offering complementary product. Example: Bought workout guide? Get nutrition guide for 50% off.
Passive Income
Income earned with minimal ongoing effort after initial setup. Examples: digital products, affiliate links, ad revenue, automated courses. Not truly "passive" but less time-intensive than active income.
Scalable
Ability to increase revenue without proportionally increasing effort/time. Digital products are scalable (sell to 1 or 1000 with same effort). 1-on-1 coaching is not scalable.

Creator Economy: Economic system where individuals monetize their content, skills, and influence directly to audience. Estimated $100B+ market.

Social Proof: Evidence that others trust you - testimonials, follower count, engagement, media mentions. Builds credibility.

Authority: Recognized expertise in your field. Built through consistent valuable content, results, and time. Essential for charging premium prices.

Personal Brand: How you're perceived by others. Combination of your expertise, personality, values, and unique positioning.

Viral Coefficient: Average number of new users each existing user brings. Above 1.0 = exponential growth.
